    • Our solar system

    Sun

    Description

    • The Sun is the star at the center of our Solar System.
    • It provides light and heat, making life possible on Earth.
    • Composed mainly of hydrogen and helium gases.
    • Key Facts: 0 moons, diameter ~1.39 million km, surface temperature ~5,500°C.
    • Source of solar energy, sunspots, and solar flares that affect planets.

    Mercury

    Description

    • Mercury is the smallest planet and closest to the Sun.
    • It has a rocky surface covered with craters.
    • Very hot during the day and extremely cold at night.
    • Key Facts: 0 moons, 58.6 Earth days per rotation, average temperature ~167°C.

    Venus

    Description

    • Venus is similar in size to Earth but has a thick toxic atmosphere.
    • Known as the “morning star” or “evening star.”
    • Surface is hidden by dense clouds of sulfuric acid.
    • Key Facts: 0 moons, 243 Earth days per rotation, average temperature ~464°C.

    Moon

    Description

    • The Moon is Earth’s only natural satellite.
    • It has a rocky, cratered surface and no atmosphere.
    • Controls tides on Earth through its gravitational pull.
    • Key Facts: 0 moons (itself), 27.3 days per rotation, average temperature ~-20°C to -173°C.
    • Explored by humans during the Apollo missions, starting in 1969.

    Earth

    Description

    • Earth is the only planet known to support life.
    • 70% of its surface is covered with water.
    • Has a breathable atmosphere and moderate climate.
    • Key Facts: 1 moon, 24 hours per rotation, average temperature ~15°C.

    Mars

    Description

    • Mars is called the Red Planet due to iron oxide on its surface.
    • Home to the largest volcano and canyon in the Solar System.
    • Possibility of past water makes it a focus for exploration.
    • Key Facts: 2 moons, 24.6 hours per rotation, average temperature ~-60°C.

    Jupiter

    Description

    • Jupiter is the largest planet in our Solar System.
    • It’s a gas giant with a prominent Great Red Spot storm.
    • Has dozens of moons and a faint ring system.
    • Key Facts: 95 moons, 9.9 hours per rotation, average temperature ~-145°C.

    Saturn

    Description

    • Saturn is famous for its extensive and bright ring system.
    • A gas giant with many moons, including Titan.
    • Less dense than water; could float if there were a large enough ocean.
    • Key Facts: 83 moons, 10.7 hours per rotation, average temperature ~-178°C.

    Uranus

    Description

    • Uranus is an ice giant with a pale blue-green color due to methane.
    • Rotates on its side, making its seasons extreme.
    • Atmosphere is composed mainly of hydrogen, helium, and methane.
    • Key Facts: 27 moons, 17.2 hours per rotation, average temperature ~-197°C.

    Neptune

    Description

    • Neptune is an ice giant and very windy with strong storms.
    • Deep blue color comes from methane in its atmosphere.
    • Farthest planet from the Sun in our system.
    • Key Facts: 14 moons, 16.1 hours per rotation, average temperature ~-201°C.

    Dwarf Planet

    Pluto

    Description

    • Pluto is a small icy world in the Kuiper Belt.
    • Known for its heart-shaped region, Sputnik Planitia.
    • Surface has mountains, plains, and frozen nitrogen.
    • Key Facts: 5 moons, 153.3 hours per rotation, average temperature ~-229°C.

    Eris

    Description

    • Eris is one of the largest known dwarf planets in our Solar System, slightly smaller than Pluto.
    • It orbits far beyond Neptune in a region called the scattered disk.
    • Its surface is extremely bright, likely covered with frozen methane.
    • Key Facts: 1 moon (Dysnomia), rotation period 25.9 hours, average temperature ~-231°C.

    Haumea

    Description

    • Haumea is an oval-shaped dwarf planet that spins very quickly on its axis.
    • Its fast rotation causes it to have a stretched, football-like shape.
    • Haumea also has a faint ring — rare among known dwarf planets.
    • Key Facts: 2 moons (Hiʻiaka and Namaka), rotation 3.9 hours, average temperature ~-241°C.
