    Missions

    Our famous space missions

    Apollo 11

    Agency: NASA
    First mission to land humans on the Moon. Astronauts Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin walked on the lunar surface. Objective: Manned lunar landing and safe return to Earth. Launch Date: July 16, 1969.

    Curiosity

    Agency: NASA
    Robotic rover exploring Mars’ surface since 2012.
    Studies the planet’s climate, geology, and habitability.
    Objective: Search for signs of past life and water.
    Launch Date: November 26, 2011.

    JWST

    Agency: NASA / ESA / CSA
    The most powerful space telescope ever built.
    Observes galaxies, stars, and exoplanets in infrared light.
    Objective: Study the early universe and planet formation.
    Launch Date: December 25, 2021.

    Artemis I

    Agency: NASA
    Uncrewed test flight around the Moon as part of Artemis program.
    Prepares future crewed lunar missions.
    Objective: Test the Space Launch System and Orion spacecraft.
    Launch Date: November 16, 2022.

    Voyager 1

    Agency: NASA
    Deep space probe traveling beyond our Solar System.
    Carries the “Golden Record” with sounds and images of Earth.
    Objective: Study outer planets and interstellar space.
    Launch Date: September 5, 1977.

    Luna 2

    Agency: Soviet Union (USSR)
    First human-made object to reach the Moon.
    Impacted the Moon’s surface in 1959.
    Objective: Study lunar surface and space environment.
    Launch Date: September 12, 1959.

    C-Huygens

    Agency: NASA / ESA / ASI
    Orbited Saturn and studied its rings and moons for 13 years.
    Huygens probe landed on Titan, Saturn’s largest moon.
    Objective: Study Saturn’s system in detail.
    Launch Date: October 15, 1997.

    H.S.T

    Agency: NASA / ESA
    Famous space telescope orbiting Earth since 1990.
    Captured stunning images of galaxies and nebulae.
    Objective: Observe the universe in visible and ultraviolet light.
    Launch Date: April 24, 1990.

    ISS

    Agency: NASA / Roscosmos / ESA / JAXA / CSA
    A permanently inhabited space laboratory in low Earth orbit.
    Collaboration between multiple space agencies.
    Objective: Conduct scientific research in microgravity.
    Launch Date: First module launched November 20, 1998.

    P.Rover

    Agency: NASA
    Latest Mars rover designed to collect rock and soil samples.
    Equipped with a drone named Ingenuity for aerial exploration.
    Objective: Search for ancient microbial life on Mars.
    Launch Date: July 30, 2020.
